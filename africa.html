Awesome Places in Africa

  • Serengeti National Park
    Bjørn Christian Tørrissen/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 3.0

    The Serengeti National Park is a Tanzanian national park in the Serengeti ecosystem. It is famous for its annual migration of over a million white-bearded wildebeests and zebras and for its numerous Nile crocodiles and honey badgers.

  • Pyramids of Giza
    Ricardo Liberato/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 2.0

    The pyramids of Giza are found on the outskirts of Cairo, Egypt. Among the ancient Seven Wonders of the World, it is the oldest and the only one still in existence.

  • Victoria Falls
    otsuka88/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Victoria Falls is a waterfall in southern Africa on the Zambezi River at the border of Zambia and Zimbabwe. It is classified as the largest in the world based on its combined width. Activities in the area include whitewater rafting, bungee jumping and game fishing.

  • Big Hole
    Redkite/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Big Hole, also known as Kimberley Hole, is an open-pit and underground diamond mine in Kimberley, South Africa. It is claimed to be the largest hole excavated by hand. It's mining operations have ceased and is now a tourist site.

  • Mount Kilimanjaro
    GregMontani/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Mount Kilimanjaro is a dormant volcano in Tanzania. It has three volcanic cones. It is a major climbing destination and has been the subject of many scientific studies because of its shrinking glaciers.

  • Fish River Canyon
    tracyhammond/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domaint

    The Fish River Canyon is located in the south of Namibia. It features a gigantic ravine and is the largest canyon in Africa. The canyon is a popular hiking site.

  • Ngorongoro Crater
    SajjadF/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 3.0

    Ngorongoro Crater is the world's largest inactive, unfilled and intact volcanic caldera. The area is inhabited by hippopotamus, lions, elephants and many others. There is a picnic site here open to tourists.

  • The Great Sphinx
    iphylglad/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Great Sphinx is a limestone statue of a mythical creature with the body of a lion and the head of a human. It stands on the Giza Plateau in Egypt. It is the oldest known monumental sculpture in Egypt.

  • Amphitheatre
    Rudolph Botha/ Wikipedia/ Public Domain

    The Amphitheatre in Drakensberg is widely known as one of the most impressive cliff faces on Earth. It is found in South Africa. The Tugela Falls, the world's second tallest falls, plunge from the Amphitheatre's cliff tops.

  • Dallol Salt Springs
    Hervé Sthioul/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 2.5

    Dallol is a volcanic explosion crater in Ethiopia. Numerous hot springs are discharging brine and acidic liquid here. Small, widespread, temporary geysers produce cones of salt.

  • Avenue of the Baobabs
    Gavinevans/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 3.0

    Avenue of the Baobabs is a group of baobab trees lining a dirt road in Madagascar. It has been a center of local conservation efforts and tourism despite not being a national park yet.

  • Temple of Seti I
    Zanaq/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 3.0

    The Temple of Seti I is the memorial temple of the New Kingdom Pharaoh Seti I. It is located in the Theban Necropolis in Upper Egypt. The ruler reigned for only two years and did not make a temple for himself.

  • Great Zimbabwe Ruins
    Jan Derk/ Wikipedia/ Public Domain

    Great Zimbabwe is a ruined city in the south-eastern hills of Zimbabwe. It is believed to have served as a royal palace for the local monarch during the country's Late Iron Age. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Axum Obelisk
    Ondřej Žváček/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 2.5

    The Axum Obelisk is a 1,700-year-old, 24-meter-tall granite stele/obelisk in Axum, Ethiopia. It is believed to function as a grave marker. It was sent to Rome for repairs and was returned in 1967.

  • Cave of Swimmers
    Roland Unger/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 3.0

    The Cave of Swimmers is an Egyptian cave with ancient rock art depicting people with their limbs bent as if they were swimming. The cave is mentioned in Michael Ondaatje's novel, which also has a film adaptation.

  • Great Mosque of Djenne
    Andy Gilham/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 3.0

    The Great Mosque of Djenne is a large adobe building. It is considered to be one of the greatest achievements of the Sudano-Sahelian architectural style. The walls are made of sun-baked earth bricks.
