Awesome Places in South America

  • Machu Picchu
    LoggaWiggler/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel situated on a mountain ridge 2,430 metres above sea level. It is located in the Cusco Region in Peru. In 2007, Machu Picchu was voted one of the New Seven Wonders of the World in a worldwide Internet poll.

  • Mano del Desierto
    hbieser/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Mano del Desierto is a large-scale sculpture of a hand located in the Atacama Desert in Chile. The sculpture was constructed by the Chilean sculptor Mario Irarrázabal. Its exaggerated size is said to emphasize human vulnerability and helplessness.

  • Rapa Nui
    myeviajes/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Rapa Nui, also known as "Easter Island", is a Chilean island in the southeastern Pacific Ocean. It is famous for its 887 extant monumental statues, called moai. The moai are chiefly the living faces of deified ancestors of the Rapa Nui people between 1250 and 1500 AD.

  • Iguazu Falls
    nanmartinav/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Iguazu Falls are waterfalls of the Iguazu River on the border of the Argentine province of Misiones and the Brazilian state of Paraná. They are the largest waterfalls system in the world. Walkways allow close views of the falls from both Argentina and Brazil.

  • Galapagos Islands
    sebastian_photos/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Galapagos Islands are an archipelago of volcanic islands in Ecuador. The islands are known for their vast number of endemic species and were studied by Charles Darwin. His observations contributed to the inception of "The Theory of Evolution".

  • Nazca Lines
    monikawl999/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Nazca Lines are a series of ancient geoglyphs in the Nazca Desert, in southern Peru. Some theories purport that the figures were made by extraterrestrial (alien) life. Scholars, in general, ascribe religious significance to them.

  • Amazon River
    Jorge.kike.medina/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 3.0

    The Amazon River is the largest river by discharge of water in the world. Its giant tropical forest houses more than one-third of all known species in the world. It is also home to many indigenous tribes.

  • Christ the Redeemer Statue
    Gustavo Facci/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 2.0

    Christ the Redeemer is an Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. A symbol of Christianity across the world, the statue has also become a cultural icon of both Rio de Janeiro and Brazil, and is listed as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

  • Salar de Uyuni
    Chechevere/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 3.0

    Salar de Uyuni is the world's largest salt flat. The Salar was formed as a result of transformations between several prehistoric lakes. Aside from being a renowned tourist spot in Bolivia, it is a major transport route and a major breeding ground for several species of flamingos.

  • Torres del Paine
    Backpackerin/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Torres del Paine are the distinctive three granite peaks of the Paine mountain range in Chile. The national park also boasts valleys, rivers such as the Paine, lakes, and glaciers.

  • Lake Titicaca
    213852/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Lake Titicaca is a large, deep lake in the Andes on the border of Peru and Bolivia. By volume of water and by surface area, it is the largest lake in South America. It is often called the "highest navigable lake" in the world.

  • Perito Moreno Glacier
    MonicaVolpin/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Perito Moreno Glacier is located in the Los Glaciares National Park in southwest Santa Cruz Province, Argentina. This ice field is the world's third largest reserve of fresh water. The glacier is unusual in that it is advancing, while most glaciers worldwide are retreating.

  • Laguna Colorada
    Carlos Adampol Galindo/ Flickr/ CC BY-SA 2.0

    Laguna Colorada is a shallow salt lake in Bolivia. It contains borax islands, whose white color contrasts with the reddish color of its waters, which is caused by red sediments and pigmentation of some algae. James's flamingos abound in the area.

  • Caño Cristales
    Mario Carvajal/ Wikimedia Commons/ CC BY 3.0

    Caño Cristales, popularly known as the "River of Five Colors", is often referred to as the most beautiful river in Colombia and even the world due to its striking colors. The bed of river in the end of July through November is variously colored yellow, green, blue, black and red.

  • Las Lajas Sanctuary
    GameOfLight/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 3.0

    Las Lajas Sanctuary is a basilica church located in southern Colombia. The present church was built in Gothic Revival style. The inspiration for the church's creation was a miraculous apparition of the Virgin Mary.

  • Angel Falls
    Luis Carillo/ Wikimedia Commons/ CC BY 3.0

    Angel Falls is the world's highest uninterrupted waterfall. It is one of Venezuela's top tourist attractions despite being located in an isolated jungle.
