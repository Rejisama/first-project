Awesome Places in South Asia

  • Taj Mahal
    Dasagani/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India. It was commissioned by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in 1632. It was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site for being considered as "the jewel of Muslim art in India."

  • Swayambhunath
    Rabs003/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 4.0

    Swayambhunath is an ancient religious architecture in Kathmandu Valley, Nepal. It is considered as a sacred Buddhist pilgrimage site. It is also known as the Monkey Temple because there are monkeys living in the temple.

  • Akshardham
    Kapil.xerox/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 4.0

    Akshardham is a Hindu temple and campus in New Delhi, India. It is believed by followers as a temporal house of God on earth. Its massive complex is intricately carved with fauna, flora, musicians, dancers and deities.

  • Pakistan Monument
    Shahid Razzaq/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 3.0

    The Pakistan Monument is a heritage museum and national monument in Islamabad, Pakistan. It was created to symbolize the country's national unity. It is also a popular picnic destination. The structure comprises four granite blossoming flower petals, which are decorated with murals.

  • Lodhi Gardens
    AKS.9955/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 4.0

    Lodhi Gardens is a park in New Delhi, India. It contains tombs and architectural works of the Lodhis, who ruled parts of northern India and some parts of Pakistan in the 15th century. At present, is a hotspot for morning walks.

  • Dudhsagar Falls
    Bahnfrend/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 3.0

    Dudhsagar Falls is a four-tiered waterfall in India. It forms the border between the states of Karnataka and Goa. The area has deciduous forests with a rich biodiversity. The falls are fed by rains giving it a huge force of water.

  • Mirissa Beach
    Dan Lundberg/ Wikimedia/ CC BY-SA 2.0

    Mirissa is a small town in southern Sri Lanka. Its beach is a popular destination for tourists who are into dolphin and whale watching. It is also popular for its night life and fishing port.

  • Sundarbans
    hasan_421517/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Sundarbans is a natural region in Bangladesh. It is the world's largest single block of tidal halophytic mangrove forest. This UNESCO World Heritage Site also has the largest reserves of Bengal tigers.

  • Hawa Mahal
    sharonang/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Hawa Mahal, which means "Palace of Winds", is a palace in Jaipur, India. Its facade is akin to the honeycomb of a beehive. It was built so the women of the royal household in the past could observe the street festivals but remain unseen from the outside.

  • Red Fort
    sharonang/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Red Fort is a historical fort in Delhi, India. It got its name for its massive red sandstone enclosing walls. It was the main residence of the Mughal dynasty emperors. At present, it houses a number of museums and is one of India's most popular tourist destinations.

  • Lotus Temple
    harmeet9000/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Lotus Temple is a house of worship in Delhi, India. It is notable for its flowerlike shape. As a Bahai house of worship, it is open to people of any religion. It has been featured in many magazine and newspaper articles and has won numerous architectural awards.

  • Elephanta Caves
    Ricardo Martins/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 2.0

    Elephanta Caves are a network of sculpted caves on Elephanta Island in Mumbai, India. The caves contain rock sculptures dedicated to the Hindu "destroyer and transformer" god Shiva. They also include square pillars and massive figures of other Hindu divinities.
