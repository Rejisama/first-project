Awesome Places in the Middle East

  • Kaaba
    ziedkammoun/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Kaaba is a building at the center of Al-Masjid al-Haram, Islam's most sacred mosque. It is found in Mecca, Saudi Arabia. One of the Five Pillars of Islam requires every Muslim (who can do so) to perform the hajj pilgrimage in Kaaba at least once in their lifetime.

  • Dead Sea
    GidonPico/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Dead Sea is a salt lake bordered by Israel and Palestine to the west and Jordan to the east. It is the deepest hypersaline lake in the world. Plants and animals cannot flourish because of its salinity, hence the name.

  • Dome of the Rock
    best-biketours/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Dome of the Rock is an Islamic shrine located in the Old City of Jerusalem. At its heart is the Foundation Stone, which bears great significance for Muslims and Jews as the site of Abraham's attempted sacrifice of his son, Ishmael.

  • Burj Khalifa
    BS1920/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Burj Khalifa is a megatall skyscraper in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. The building broke numerous height records and received many awards, including its designation as the tallest building in the world.

  • Great Mosque of Samarra
    Jim Gordon - Flickr/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 2.0

    The Great Mosque of Samarra is a ninth-century mosque located in Iraq. It was, for a time, the largest mosque in the world. It was destroyed in 1278 after Hulagu Khan's invasion of Iraq. Only the minaret and outer wall remain.

  • Ein Gedi
    צילום: נצח פרביאש/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 2.5

    Ein Gedi is a nature reserve and oasis in Israel. The reserve is a sanctuary for many types of animal and plant species. It also features several archaeological sites, which include the Chalcolithic Temple of Ein Gedi.

  • Ferrari World Abu Dhabi
    Aziz J.Hayat/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 2.0

    Ferrari World Abu Dhabi is an amusement park located in the United Arab Emirates. It owns the record for the largest space frame structure ever built. It also has the world's fastest roller coaster, Formula Rossa.

  • Sea of Galilee
    heathertruett/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Sea of Galilee is a large freshwater lake in Israel. It is the lowest freshwater lake on Earth. According to the Bible, it is where Jesus performed miracles such as walking on water and calming the storm; hence making it an attraction for Christian pilgrims.

  • Palm Jumeirah
    Commander Leroy Chiao/ Wikipedia/ Public Domain

    Palm Jumeirah is an artificial archipelago in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. It is one of the three planned islands, called the Palm Islands, that will host to a large number of residential and leisure centers and non-public beaches.

  • Wailing Wall
    MaciejJaszczolt/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Wailing Wall is an ancient limestone wall in the Old City of Jerusalem. Its name refers to the practice of Jews weeping and praying at the site. It is considered holy due to its connection to the Temple Mount, another important religious site behind it.

  • Umayyad Mosque
    DianneKet78/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Umayyad Mosque is one of the largest and oldest mosques in the world. It is found in the old city of Damascus. It was built on the site of a Christian basilica dedicated to John the Baptist, honored as a prophet by Christians and Muslims.

  • Golestan Palace
    Diego Delso/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 4.0

    Golestan Palace is the former royal Persian complex of the Qajar dynasty in Tehran, Iran's capital city. It consists of royal buildings, gardens, and collections of Iranian crafts and European presents.
