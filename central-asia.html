Awesome Places in Central Asia

  • Genghis Khan Statue
    czu_czu_PL/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Genghis Khan Equestrian Statue is a 40-metre statue of Genghis Khan, founder of the Mongol Empire, in Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia. It is symbolically pointed east towards his birthplace. The attached museum has exhibitions relating to the archaeological cultures in Mongolia.

  • Registan Square
    Ekrem Canli/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 3.0

    Registan Square was the heart of the ancient city of Samarkand of the Timurid dynasty in Uzbekistan. This present-day tourist site was a public square where people gathered to hear royal proclamations and see public executions in the past.

  • Door to Hell
    Tormod Sandtorv/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 2.0

    The Darvaza gas crater, known locally as "Door to Hell", is a natural gas field with the size of an American football field in Turkmenistan. This tourist site collapsed into an underground cavern in 1971 and has been burning continuously since geologists set it aflame to prevent the spread of methane gas.

  • Bayterek Tower
    ChelseaFunNumberOne/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 4.0

    Bayterek Tower is a monument and observation tower in Astana, Kazakhstan. It was built to embody a folktale about a tree of life and a magical bird of happiness. Its observation deck is 97 meter above ground level, which corresponds to 1997, the year that Astana became the country's capital.

  • Khan Shatyr
    Albert Bergonzo/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 4.0

    Khan Shatyr is a is a giant transparent tent with a neofuturist style in Astana, Kazakhstan. Underneath it are various facilities including an urban-scale internal park, a shopping and entertainment venue, a boating river, and a minigolf and indoor beach resort.

  • Bibi-Khanym Mosque
    Bobyrr/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 4.0

    The Bibi-Khanym Mosque was one of the most magnificent and largest mosques in the Islamic world in the 15th century. It collapsed due to an earthquake in 1897 but major parts of the mosque have been restored at present.

  • Ark of Bukhara
    RK/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 4.0

    Ark of Bukhara is a massive earthen fortress built around the 5th century AD in Bukhara, Uzbekistan. Aside from being a military structure, it was a town with royal courts. At present, the Ark houses museums and serves as a tourist attraction.

  • Ala Archa National Park
    Garthwillis/ Wikipedia/ CC BY 2.5

    Ala Archa National Park is found in the Tian Shan mountains of Kyrgyzstan. It is a popular destination point for hikers, picnickers, skiers, horse trekkers and mountain climbers. The alpine park includes a beautiful river gorge and the very rare snow leopard.

  • Ile-Alatau National Park
    ru:User:Rise2Rise/ Wikipedia/ CC BY-SA 3.0

    Ile-Alatau National Park is located in Kazakhstan. The landscape includes alpine meadows, glaciers, woodlands, and lakes, including Big Almaty Lake. It is home to a total of 300 species of animals and birds.
