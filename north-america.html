Awesome Places in North America

  • Grand Canyon
    DomCarver/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Grand Canyon is a steep-sided canyon carved by the Colorado River in the state of Arizona in the United States. Popular activities include sightseeing, skydiving, rafting, hiking, running, and helicopter tours.

  • Chichen Itza
    CSITDMS/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Chichen Itza was a large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya people of the Terminal Classic period. The archaeological site is located in Tinúm Municipality, Yucatán State, Mexico.

  • Yellowstone National Park
    tpsdave/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Yellowstone National Park is located in the U.S. states of Wyoming, Montana and Idaho. It is known for its wildlife, geothermal features, lakes, canyons, rivers and mountain ranges.

  • Mount Rushmore
    skeeze/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    This sculpture is carved into the granite face of Black Hills in Keystone, South Dakota, United States. It features the heads of four US presidents: George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Theodore Roosevelt and Abraham Lincoln.

  • Niagara Falls
    ataribravo99/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Niagara Falls is the collective name for three waterfalls that straddle the international border between Canada and the United States. It is famed both for its beauty and as a valuable source of hydroelectric power.

  • El Arco
    liezelzpineda/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    El Arco is a distinctive rock formation at the southern tip of Cabo San Lucas, the extreme southern end of Mexico's Baja California Peninsula. This spot is also a popular gathering area for sea lions.

  • Teotihuacan
    jjnanni/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Teotihuacan is an ancient Mesoamerican city located in a sub-valley of the Valley of Mexico. It has pyramids, multi-family residential compounds, the Avenue of the Dead, and vibrant murals.

  • Moraine Lake
    connormik/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Moraine Lake is a glacially fed lake in Banff National Park, Alberta, Canada. When it is full, it reflects a distinctive shade of blue. The colour is due to the refraction of light off the rock flour deposited in the lake on a continual basis.

  • Central Park
    Unsplash/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Central Park is an urban park in Manhattan, New York City. It is the most visited urban park in the United States and one of the most filmed locations in the world.

  • Panama Canal
    skeeze/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Panama Canal is an artificial waterway in Panama that connects the Atlantic Ocean with the Pacific Ocean. The canal cuts across the Isthmus of Panama and is a key conduit for international maritime trade.

  • Yosemite National Park
    tpsdave/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    This national park spans portions of Tuolumne, Mariposa and Madera counties in Northern California. It is internationally recognized for its granite cliffs, waterfalls, clear streams, giant sequoia groves, lakes, mountains, glaciers, and biological diversity.

  • Golden Gate Bridge
    tpsdave/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Golden Gate Bridge is a suspension bridge spanning the Golden Gate strait, the channel between San Francisco Bay and the Pacific Ocean. The bridge is one of the most internationally recognized symbols of San Francisco, California, and the United States.

  • Pikes Peak
    RescueWarrior/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Pikes Peak is the highest summit of the southern Front Range of the Rocky Mountains, in North America. The mountain is named in honor of American explorer Zebulon Pike, who was unable to reach the summit. It is composed of a characteristic pink granite.

  • Empire State Building
    Unsplash/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    The Empire State Building is a 102-story skyscraper located on Fifth Avenue between West 33rd and 34th Streets in Midtown, Manhattan, New York City. It stood as the world's tallest building for nearly 40 years until the late 1970s.

  • Montezuma Castle
    JesterGuy198181/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Montezuma Castle is situated about 90 feet up a sheer limestone cliff in Camp Verde, Arizona, United States. The dwellings were built and used by the Sinagua people, a pre-Columbian culture.

  • Statue of Liberty
    skeeze/ Pixabay/ CC0 Public Domain

    Statue of Liberty is a colossal neoclassical sculpture on Liberty Island in New York City. The statue is of a robed female figure representing Libertas, the Roman goddess. The statue was constructed in France, shipped overseas in crates, and assembled on the completed pedestal.
